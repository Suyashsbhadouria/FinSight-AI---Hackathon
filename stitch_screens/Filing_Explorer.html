Item 1A. Risk Factors

Our business, financial condition, and results of operations could be adversely affected by any of the following risks. The risks described below are not the only ones we face. Additional risks and uncertainties not currently known to us or that we currently deem to be immaterial may also materially and adversely affect our business.

Strategic Supply Chain Vulnerabilities

We rely on a limited number of specialized suppliers for certain critical components and manufacturing services. Specifically, our dependence on TSMC for advanced semiconductor fabrication and ASML for lithography systems creates significant concentration risk. Any disruption in the operations of these partners, whether due to geopolitical tensions in the Taiwan Strait or trade restrictions, could severely impact our production capacity.

report Material Risk Detected

"The increasing complexity of advanced packaging technologies requires closer collaboration with third-party foundries. Failure to secure priority access to these technologies may lead to product launch delays and loss of market share to competitors."

Furthermore, the ongoing global shift toward sovereign semiconductor manufacturing capabilities has led to increased regulatory scrutiny. Changes in export control regulations, particularly regarding high-performance computing (HPC) chips, may limit our ability to service clients in specific jurisdictions, potentially impacting our long-term revenue projections in the APAC region.
